Engineering Challenge 5-Day Summer Program

The Slow-Motion Ball Run Challenge

Can you design a paper ball run that keeps a ball moving for as long as possible?

Your team will design, build, test, and improve a paper ball run. Your goal is not to make the ball move the fastest. Your goal is to keep it moving for as long as possible before it reaches the exit.

Challenge

The engineering problem

Engineers solve problems inside real limits. Read the mission, criteria, and constraints carefully. Then use the guided questions to check that you understand the challenge.

Mission

Design and build a paper ball run that keeps a ball moving for as long as possible. The ball must enter at the marked entrance, follow the track, and reach the exit without help.

Criteria (What success looks like)

  • The ball travels for as long as possible during a successful run.
  • Your best design is reliable across many trials.
  • The structure stands up on its own.
  • The path is clearly labeled with an entrance and an exit.

Constraints (Rules you must follow)

  • Build only with approved paper and tape.
  • You cannot touch the ball or the structure during a timed run.
  • A ball that stays stuck for 10 seconds counts as unsuccessful.
  • Every team gets the same starting materials.
Slow is the goal. Speed is the trap. A design that keeps the ball moving without stopping will beat a design that finishes quickly.

Learn

Concepts you will use

You do not need to memorize these. You need to be able to use them when you design, build, and improve your ball run.

Gravitational Potential Energy

When the ball is high, it stores energy. The higher your entrance, the more energy the ball has to spend on the trip.

Kinetic Energy

Moving energy. Your track shapes how kinetic energy is used up over time.

Friction

A push against motion. Some friction slows the ball down usefully. Too much friction can stop the ball completely.

Structure and Support

Your run has to hold itself up. If the track sags, the ball may leave the path or stop.

Engineering Design Process

Define, Imagine, Plan, Build, Test, Improve, Repeat. Real engineers loop through this cycle many times.

Iteration

A planned change based on evidence. Not a random change. Each iteration is one new version of your design.

Fair Testing

Test in the same way every time. Same release point. Same ball. Same setup. That is how results become trustworthy.

Reliability

How often your design works. A run that succeeds 8 out of 10 times is more reliable than one that succeeds 2 out of 10.

Optimization

Making a design as good as possible for the goal. Here, that means as long a travel time as possible while still being reliable.
