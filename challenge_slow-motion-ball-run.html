Engineering Challenge

Slow-Motion Ball Run
Challenge

🔍
Driving Question
Can you design a paper ball run that keeps a ping pong ball moving for as long as possible?

Your team will design, build, test, and improve a paper ball run over five days. Your goal is not to make the ball move the fastest. Your goal is to keep it moving for as long as possible before it reaches the exit.

Engineering Challenge

The engineering problem

Every engineering challenge has a goal, criteria for success, and constraints you must respect. Read these carefully before Day 1.

Goal

Design and build a paper ball run that keeps a ball moving for as long as possible. The ball must enter at the marked entrance, follow the track, and reach the exit without help.

Criteria

  • The ball travels for as long as possible during a successful run.
  • Your best design is reliable across many trials.
  • The structure stands up on its own.
  • The path is clearly labeled with an entrance and an exit.

Constraints

  • Build only with approved paper and tape.
  • You cannot touch the ball or the structure during a timed run.
  • A ball that stays stuck for 10 seconds counts as unsuccessful.
  • Every team gets the same starting materials.
Slow is the goal. Speed is the trap. A design that keeps the ball moving without stopping will beat a design that finishes quickly.
What You'll Learn

Skills you will build

This challenge builds engineering habits you will use across science and beyond.

Engineering Design Process

Define, imagine, plan, build, test, improve, repeat. Real engineers loop through this cycle many times.

Iteration

A planned change based on evidence, not a random change. Each iteration is one new version of your design.

Fair Testing

Test the same way every time. Same release point, same ball, same setup. That is how results become trustworthy.

Evidence

Use observations and measurements from your trials to explain why one design works better than another.

Optimization

Make a design as good as possible for the goal. Here, that means as long a travel time as possible while staying reliable.

Teamwork

Share ideas, share roles, share credit. The best engineering happens in teams that trust each other.

Your Five-Day Journey

Five days of engineering

Each day builds on the last. Start with Day 1 and follow the sequence, or jump to any day you need.

Day 1

Understand the Challenge

Meet the problem. Learn the vocabulary. Sketch a first plan.

Day 2

Build and Test

Turn your plan into a prototype. Run your first timed trials.

Day 3

Analyze and Improve

Study your results. Find the main problem. Redesign with evidence.

Day 4

Optimize the Design

Tune the details. Make the design more reliable and longer-lasting.

Day 5

Communicate and Reflect

Share your final design. Explain what you learned as an engineer.
