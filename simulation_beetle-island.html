Beetle Island

Which beetles would survive best on this island — and how would the population change over time? Set the environment and find out.

🧬 The Science Behind the Simulation
Variation Exists

The beetle population starts with two coat colors — light and dark. This natural variation comes from inherited traits passed down through generations. No two beetles are identical.

Environment Applies Pressure

Beetles that blend into their environment are harder for predators to spot — they survive longer and reproduce more. Beetles that stand out get eaten before they can pass on their traits.

Traits Are Inherited

Survivors pass their coat color to offspring. Over many generations, the trait that helps survival becomes more common in the population — this is natural selection at work.

⚠️ Common Misconception

Beetles don't choose to change color. Individual beetles stay the same their whole lives. It's the population that changes — because survivors reproduce and pass on their traits.

⚄ Even well-camouflaged beetles don't survive every generation — chance affects each survival roll.
