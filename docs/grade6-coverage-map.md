# Grade 6 STE Standards Coverage Map

Audit pass for LyfeLabz. Assesses readiness toward a comprehensive Grade 6
core curriculum (eventual EdReports / CURATE submission).

- Source of standards: Massachusetts 2016 Science and Technology/Engineering
  Curriculum Framework, Grade 6 section (pages 54-59 of the framework PDF).
- Generated: 2026-06-30.
- This is a documentation/audit pass only. No lesson or page files were modified.

## Grade 6 integration theme (from the framework)

**Structure and Function.** The integration of Earth and space, life, and
physical sciences with technology/engineering gives Grade 6 students
opportunities with natural phenomena and design problems that highlight the
relationship of structure and function. Students relate structure and function
by analyzing the macro- and microscopic world (Earth features and processes,
the role of cells and anatomy, and properties of materials and waves). Students
use models and provide evidence to make claims about structure-function
relationships across STE domains.

There are **22** standards coded with a `6.` prefix, across four disciplines:
Earth and Space Science (4), Life Science (5), Physical Science (7), and
Technology/Engineering (6).

## Step 1 - Authoritative Grade 6 standard list

### Earth and Space Sciences (6.MS-ESS)

- **6.MS-ESS1-1a** Develop and use a model of the Earth-Sun-Moon system to
  explain the causes of lunar phases and eclipses of the Sun and Moon.
  *Clarification:* models can be physical, graphical, or conceptual and should
  emphasize relative positions and distances.
- **6.MS-ESS1-4** Analyze and interpret rock layers and index fossils to
  determine the relative ages of rock formations that result from processes
  occurring over long periods of time.
  *Clarification:* includes laws of superposition and crosscutting relationships
  (minor displacement faults); long-term processes include weathering, erosion,
  heat, and pressure.
  *Assessment boundary:* reordered/overturned strata, named periods/epochs, or
  naming minerals/rock types are not expected.
- **6.MS-ESS1-5(MA)** Use graphical displays to illustrate that Earth and its
  solar system are one of many in the Milky Way galaxy, which is one of billions
  of galaxies in the universe.
  *Clarification:* displays can include maps, charts, graphs, and data tables.
- **6.MS-ESS2-3** Analyze and interpret maps showing the distribution of fossils
  and rocks, continental shapes, and seafloor structures to provide evidence
  that Earth's plates have moved great distances, collided, and spread apart.
  *Clarification:* maps similar to Wegener's visuals (rock/fossil similarity,
  continental shapes including shelves, ocean structures such as ridges, fracture
  zones, trenches).
  *Assessment boundary:* mechanisms for plate motion or paleomagnetic anomalies
  are not expected.

### Life Science (6.MS-LS)

- **6.MS-LS1-1** Provide evidence that all organisms (unicellular and
  multicellular) are made of cells.
- **6.MS-LS1-2** Develop and use a model to describe how parts of cells
  contribute to the cellular functions of obtaining food/water/nutrients,
  disposing of wastes, and providing energy.
  *Clarification:* nucleus, chloroplasts, mitochondria, vacuoles, cell membrane,
  cell wall.
  *Assessment boundary:* specific biochemical steps, the role of ATP, active
  transport, or comparing cell types are not expected.
- **6.MS-LS1-3** Construct an argument supported by evidence that the body
  systems interact to carry out essential functions of life.
  *Clarification:* circulatory, digestive, respiratory, excretory,
  muscular/skeletal, and nervous systems; emphasis on interactions, not parts.
  *Assessment boundary:* mechanism of one system in isolation or biochemical
  processes are not expected.
- **6.MS-LS4-1** Analyze and interpret evidence from the fossil record to
  describe organisms and their environment, extinctions, and changes to life
  forms throughout Earth's history.
  *Assessment boundary:* names of individual species, geological eras, or
  mechanisms for speciation are not expected.
- **6.MS-LS4-2** Construct an argument using anatomical structures to support
  evolutionary relationships among and between fossil and modern organisms.

### Physical Science (6.MS-PS)

- **6.MS-PS1-6** Plan and conduct an experiment involving exothermic and
  endothermic chemical reactions to measure and describe the release or
  absorption of thermal energy.
  *Clarification:* emphasis on transfer of energy to/from the environment.
- **6.MS-PS1-7(MA)** Use a particulate model of matter to explain that density
  is the amount of matter (mass) in a given volume. Apply proportional reasoning
  to describe, calculate, and compare relative densities.
- **6.MS-PS1-8(MA)** Conduct an experiment to show that many materials are
  mixtures of pure substances that can be separated by physical means into their
  component pure substances.
  *Clarification:* examples include salt water, oil and vinegar, milk, air.
- **6.MS-PS2-4** Use evidence to support the claim that gravitational forces
  between objects are attractive and are only noticeable when one or both objects
  have a very large mass.
  *Assessment boundary:* Newton's law of gravitation or Kepler's laws are not
  expected.
- **6.MS-PS4-1** Use diagrams of a simple wave to explain that (a) a wave has a
  repeating pattern with a specific amplitude, frequency, and wavelength, and
  (b) the amplitude is related to the energy of the wave.
  *Assessment boundary:* electromagnetic waves not expected; limited to standard
  repeating waves.
- **6.MS-PS4-2** Use diagrams and other models to show that both light rays and
  mechanical waves are reflected, absorbed, or transmitted through various
  materials.
  *Assessment boundary:* limited to qualitative applications.
- **6.MS-PS4-3** Present qualitative information to support the claim that
  digitized signals (sent as wave pulses representing 0s and 1s) can be used to
  encode and transmit information.
  *Assessment boundary:* binary counting or device-specific mechanisms not
  expected.

### Technology/Engineering (6.MS-ETS)

- **6.MS-ETS1-1** Define the criteria and constraints of a design problem with
  sufficient precision to ensure a successful solution, including potential
  impacts on people and the natural environment.
- **6.MS-ETS1-5(MA)** Create visual representations of solutions to a design
  problem. Accurately interpret and apply scale and proportion.
  *Clarification:* sketches, scaled drawings, orthographic projections.
- **6.MS-ETS1-6(MA)** Communicate a design solution to an intended user,
  including design features and limitations of the solution.
- **6.MS-ETS2-1(MA)** Analyze and compare properties of metals, plastics, wood,
  and ceramics, including flexibility, ductility, hardness, thermal conductivity,
  electrical conductivity, and melting point.
- **6.MS-ETS2-2(MA)** Given a design task, select appropriate materials based on
  specific properties needed in the construction of a solution.
- **6.MS-ETS2-3(MA)** Choose and safely use appropriate measuring tools, hand
  tools, fasteners, and common hand-held power tools used to construct a
  prototype.

## Step 4 - Proposed Grade 6 unit structure

Six units, sequenced to build the "Structure and Function" through-line from the
microscopic to the macroscopic, then into materials and design. Existing pages
are listed; gaps mark what still needs to be built.

### Unit 1 - Cells: The Structure of Life
- **Standards:** 6.MS-LS1-1, 6.MS-LS1-2
- **Existing pages:** lesson_what-is-life, lesson_cell-types, lesson_organelles,
  investigation_gray-zone, investigation_cell-energy, investigation_protein-pathway,
  extension_virus, game_cell-explorer, game_cellular-showdown, game_is-it-alive
- **Gaps:** None. Strongest unit in the catalog.

### Unit 2 - Body Systems
- **Standards:** 6.MS-LS1-3
- **Existing pages:** lesson_body-systems, extension_body-systems,
  extension_neuron-explorer, game_exercise, system_*.html (8), disease_*.html (8)
- **Gaps:** None for the PE. Optional: a culminating "systems interaction"
  investigation to generate written-argument evidence.

### Unit 3 - Evolution and the Fossil Record
- **Standards:** 6.MS-LS4-1, 6.MS-LS4-2
- **Existing pages:** lesson_biological-evolution, extension_chernobyl-frogs, game_evolution-clicker
- **Gaps:** None. Keep speciation mechanism out (grade 8).

### Unit 4 - Matter and Its Properties
- **Standards:** 6.MS-PS1-6, 6.MS-PS1-7(MA), 6.MS-PS1-8(MA)
- **Existing pages:** lesson_measuring-matter, lesson_physical-properties,
  lesson_pure-substances-and-mixtures, lesson_chemical-reactions
- **Gaps:** No new lessons needed. **RESOLVED:** extension_hidden-world-of-matter

### Unit 5 - Waves and Information Transfer
- **Standards:** 6.MS-PS4-1, 6.MS-PS4-2, 6.MS-PS4-3
- **Existing pages:** lesson_nature-of-waves, lesson_wave-behavior,
  lesson_digital-signals, investigation_amplitude-challenge, game_photon-runner
- **Gaps:** None. Gold-standard unit.

### Unit 6 - Engineering, Materials, and Design
- **Standards:** 6.MS-ETS1-1, 6.MS-ETS1-5(MA), 6.MS-ETS1-6(MA),
  6.MS-ETS2-1(MA), 6.MS-ETS2-2(MA), 6.MS-ETS2-3(MA)
- **Existing pages:** lesson_engineering-design (ETS1-1, ETS1-6)
- **Gaps:** The largest gap area. Need:
  - A **scale-and-drawing** lesson (ETS1-5): sketches, scaled drawings,

  - A **material-properties** lesson (ETS2-1): metals/plastics/wood/ceramics,

  - A **material-selection** design task (ETS2-2).
  - A **tools-and-prototyping** lesson (ETS2-3): measuring tools, hand tools,
    fasteners, power tools, safety.

### Off-Grade Risk items (non-negotiable accuracy fixes)

The Massachusetts framework introduces **atoms and molecules in grade 8**
(8.MS-PS1-1 and related). Grade 6 matter standards are built on a **particulate**
model ("particles," "amount of matter"), not atomic structure. The following
Grade-6-labeled pages teach grade-8 atomic/molecular mechanism:

1. **extension_hidden-world-of-matter.html** - **RESOLVED.** Re-leveled to grade 8.
   Retitled "The Hidden World of Matter" and repositioned as a Grade 8 (8.MS-PS1-1)
   Looking Ahead extension. Removed from the Grade 6 core set on the homepage and
   now surfaces under the Grade 8 filter. Standard codes updated from 6.MS-PS1-1 to
   8.MS-PS1-1. The high-quality instructional content was preserved intact.
2. **lesson_chemical-reactions.html** - Labeled Grade 6, badged to **MS-PS1-5
   (grade 8: "atoms are rearranged")** alongside the on-grade MS-PS1-6, with ~140
   atom/molecule references. The PS1-6 energy-transfer goal is grade 6, but the
   explanatory mechanism (atomic rearrangement) is grade 8. Re-scope the
   explanation to "energy released/absorbed" and drop the PS1-5 badge for the
   grade-6 version.
3. **lesson_pure-substances-and-mixtures.html** (minor) - Labeled Grade 6,
   on-grade standard PS1-8(MA), but ~23 atom/molecule references. Verify the
   "pure substance" definition stays at the particle level and does not lean on
   atomic structure in assessed content.
