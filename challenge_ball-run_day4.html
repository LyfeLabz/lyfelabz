Optimize the Design

Today you will take your strongest design, tune it, run final trials, and prepare a clear explanation of how it works.

Today's work

Optimization means improving a design while still meeting the rules of the challenge. The best design is not always the most complicated. A strong design performs consistently, and engineers also think about reliability, simplicity, materials, and trade-offs.

Reliable beats fancy. A design that works once by luck is not as strong as a design that works nearly every time.

Learning target

I can optimize a design and use repeated trials to evaluate its performance.

By the end of today you should be able to describe your final design, show its performance data, and identify at least one trade-off.

Engineering Tip

A short tip from real engineers.

A reliable design works repeatedly, not just once. If a design only works when everything is perfect, engineers say it is not really working yet.

Real World

Where engineers use this kind of thinking.

Engineers balance performance, cost, simplicity, and safety. Bridge builders, medical device teams, and product designers all pick their final design with trade-offs in mind.
